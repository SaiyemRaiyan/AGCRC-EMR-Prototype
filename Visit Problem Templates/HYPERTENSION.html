Diagnosis
Measurement of blood pressure: No smoking or caffeine consumption during previous one hour. Empty bladder. 5 minutes resting, seated, back supported, both feet on floor, legs uncrossed, arm supported to level of heart, cuff on bare skin, with monitor, take measurements 2-3 times separated by 3 minutes between measurements. Calculate the average systolic and diastolic measurements as the blood pressure of record.
Hypertension is present if:
  • On three separate assessments over a span of at least 3-4 weeks, properly measured blood pressure is:
    • >130 mmHg systolic and/or >80 mmHg diastolic
Hypertension stage categories are:
  • Stage 1: Systolic BP 130-139 mmHg or Diastolic BP 80-89 mmHg
  • Stage 2: Systolic BP >=140 mmHg or Diastolic BP >=90 mmHg
The goal of treatment is to keep a patient's BP less than systolic 130 mmHg and diastolic 90 mmHg or 80 mm Hg in diabetic or patients with chronic renal failure in order to decrease the likelihood of stroke, heart and kidney diseases, and heart attack. For patients 65 and over, who often have isolated systolic hypertension, with stiffer blood vessels, the treatment goal is BP less than 140 systolic and 90 diastolic.
