Diagnosis
Red flags: New headache; explosive headache; change in chronic headache pattern with increasing severity; neurological symptoms or signs such as altered mental status, focal neurological deficit, stiff neck, worse headache when lying flat.
Clinical diagnosis:
  • Tension/muscle contraction type headache (H/A). Most common type. Slow onset. Tightening/squeezing around the entire head or bilateral in frontotemporal, occipito-cervical regions. Common triggers: Hunger, sleep deprivation, dehydration
  • Cluster headache (H/A). Infrequent. More common in men. Burning or stabbing pain around the eye. Unilateral.
  • Migraine headache (H/A). Headaches occurring at intervals. Throbbing pain. Nausea. Light and sound sensitivity. Usually affecting one side of head; commonly frontotemporal. More common in women and during ages 30 thru 40s. Some migraine H/As are associated with aura preceding H/A lasting 5-60 minutes with visual and sensory symptoms.
  • Post traumatic headache (H/A).
