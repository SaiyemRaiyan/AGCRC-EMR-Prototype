Diagnosis Reference

Diagnosis:

Hemoglobin A1c blood test more than 6.5% OR Random blood glucose more than 200 mg/dL OR Fasting blood glucose more than 125 mg/dL in the presence of weight loss, increased hunger, polyuria and polydipsia.

DM1 = type 1 diabetes mellitus (5-10% of cases); Autoimmune disorder against pancreatic Beta cells ‚Üí insulin deficiency.

DM2 = type 2 diabetes mellitus (90-95% of cases); Insulin resistance in target organs and relative insulin deficiency.

Diabetic ketoacidosis: Hyperglycemic emergency with history of a few days of polydipsia, polyuria, vomiting; Then dehydration, rapid deep breathing (Kussmaul respiration), fruity smelling breath, confusion, and shock. Commonly caused by infection.

Hypoglycemia in diabetics treated with insulin. Confusion, altered behavior, anxiety, sweating, palpitations, tachycardia, and tremor.
