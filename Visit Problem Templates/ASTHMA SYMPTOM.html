Definition

Cardinal triad of asthma: Intermittent shortness of breath, wheezing, and cough. If a patient has chronic cough alone, and normal spirometric measures, other allergic conditions such as allergic rhinitis, rhinosinusitis, nasal polyposis and gastroesophageal reflux should be considered.

Red Flags

Cigarette smoke exposure increases severity of asthmatic attacks

Decreasing the frequency of asthma attacks, and getting a complete response to treatment with normalization of spirometric measures, and complete absence of any wheezing on careful chest auscultation, are important goals of treatment because meeting these goals reduces the likelihood of permanent scarring of bronchioles with development of COPD.
