Diagnosis Reference

In a consecutive case series of 1085 women seeking help for breast problems in Bangladesh: Two thirds of women had history, physical examination and ultrasound findings consistent with fibrocystic conditions (diffuse tenderness and lumpiness, especially in upper outer quadrants of breasts), 12% had fibro-adenomas, 5% inflammatory-infectious conditions (abscess, nipple fungal infection, bacterial or granulomatous mastitis), 4% had well-defined cyclic mastalgia, 2% obvious or strongly suspected breast malignancy, and 1% had masses of uncertain nature.

Red Flags
  • Rapid onset of inflammatory signs → abscess, bacterial/granulomatous infection, inflammatory breast cancer.
  • Chronic, growing, irregular bordered, unilateral breast mass, especially with skin changes → suspect breast cancer.
  • Nipple discharge which is unilateral and bloody or sero-sanguineous → intraductal papilloma, ductal ectasia; with mass → breast cancer.
